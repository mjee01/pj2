Sprawled out on the couch, Leon watched Noel pack. He began to understand the rush to leave the city. The haze of the morning gave Noel’s small residence a different feeling. No traces remained of the long nights in the living room. Exam cram sessions, endless conversations, sitting at the counter watching Noel cook. Oftentimes, he’d walk into the apartment, greeted by the fragrant scent of homemade rice bowls. Leon’s favorite food, but without vegetables, so Noel wouldn’t put them on his plate. There was no echo of laughter over the background of RPG music, no lightheartedness to turn to. Now, there was just dust remaining on the shelf where Noel had taken away objects.

Noel filed through a stack of his papers, threw away a family album. As much as Leon joked about living at Noel’s place, no one shared the space with him. It was all Noel’s: the blue-brown color of his furniture, his neat tendencies, the mild smell of cleaning agent. All of the space and his deceased parents’ money.

With a responsible personality like Noel’s, Leon imagined it must have gotten pretty boring at some point.
